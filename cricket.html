Cricket - The Gentleman's Game

Discover the rich history, exciting matches, and legendary players that make cricket a global phenomenon.

About Cricket

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field at the center of which is a 22-yard (20-metre) pitch with a wicket at each end, each comprising two bails balanced on three stumps.

The game has evolved from its origins in England over 500 years ago to a global sport with various formats, including Test matches, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 (T20) games.

Did You Know?

  • The longest Test match in history lasted 14 days!
  • A cricket ball has between 65 and 78 stitches.
  • The first international cricket match was played in 1844 between the USA and Canada.

Cricket Formats

Test Cricket

The longest format of the game, played over five days between national teams. It is considered the ultimate test of a team's skill and endurance.

One Day International (ODI)

A 50-over per side format, typically completed in a single day. It offers a balance between the traditional and modern aspects of the game.

Twenty20 (T20)

The shortest format, with 20 overs per side, known for its fast-paced action and high-scoring matches. It has gained immense popularity worldwide.

T10

An even shorter format with 10 overs per side, designed for quick, action-packed matches that last about 90 minutes.

Basic Cricket Rules

Key Rules of Cricket

  • Two teams of 11 players each compete to score the most runs.
  • The batting team tries to score runs, while the fielding team attempts to bowl them out.
  • A match is divided into innings, where each team takes turns to bat.
  • The bowler delivers the ball to the batsman, who tries to hit it and score runs.
  • Runs are scored by running between the wickets or hitting the ball to the boundary.
  • A batsman can be dismissed in various ways, including being caught, bowled, or run out.
  • The team with the most runs at the end of the match wins.
