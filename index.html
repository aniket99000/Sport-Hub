Badminton - The High-Speed Racquet Sport

Badminton is a fast-paced racquet sport that demands quick reflexes, agility, and stamina. Despite its delicate appearance, the shuttlecock can reach speeds of over 300 mph, making badminton one of the fastest sports in the world.

Did You Know?

  • The fastest recorded badminton hit was 493 km/h (306 mph).
  • Badminton became an Olympic sport in 1992.
  • A badminton match can consist of up to 100 rallies.
  • Thomas and Uber Cup: The two most prestigious team tournaments in badminton are the Thomas Cup (for men) and the Uber Cup (for women). These tournaments are held every two years and feature teams from all over the world
  • Most Dominant Country: China has historically been the most dominant nation in badminton, winning numerous Olympic gold medals and world championships. However, countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and Denmark are also strong contenders in the sport.

  • Olympic Champions: Lin Dan of China is widely considered one of the greatest badminton players of all time, winning two Olympic gold medals and five World Championships in his career.
  • Number of Feathers: Each shuttlecock used in professional badminton is made from 16 feathers, typically taken from the left wing of a goose. Synthetic shuttlecocks are also used for recreational play, but feathered ones are standard in high-level competition.

Kabaddi - The Game of Raid and Defense

Kabaddi is a unique contact sport that originated in ancient India. It combines elements of wrestling and tag, requiring both physical strength and mental agility. The sport has gained international recognition and is now played in many countries around the world.

Did You Know?

  • The word "Kabaddi" is derived from the Tamil word "kai-pidi" which means "to hold hands".
  • A Kabaddi match lasts for 40 minutes with a 5-minute break in between.
  • Kabaddi made its Asian Games debut in 1990.
  • Types of Kabaddi: There are different forms of Kabaddi, such as Circle Kabaddi (played on a circular field) and Standard Kabaddi (played on a rectangular court). The standard version is more popular in international tournaments.
  • Ancient Roots: Some scholars believe that Kabaddi was played by ancient warriors to develop self-defense skills and physical fitness.
  • Popular Regions: While Kabaddi is extremely popular in India, it is also widely played in countries like Iran, Bangladesh, Pakistan, South Korea, and Japan.
